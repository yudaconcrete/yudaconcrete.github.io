What Is a Usina de Concreto Móvel?

A mobile usina de concreto is a compact, wheeled concrete production unit designed for rapid deployment and easy relocation. Unlike stationary plants that require concrete foundations and permanent installation, a mobile plant can be towed to site, set up in days, and start producing quality concrete immediately. It integrates the mixing system, aggregate bins, cement silo, weighing system, and control cabin on a single chassis — making it the ideal choice for projects with short construction periods or multiple work sites.

Aplicações

Road & Highway Construction

Mobile plants are widely used for road and highway projects, where concrete is needed at multiple points along the route. Simply move the plant as construction progresses.

Bridge & Tunnel Projects

Remote bridge and tunnel construction sites benefit from on-site concrete production, reducing transportation time and ensuring fresh concrete delivery.

Rural & Urban Infrastructure

Village roads, drainage projects, and utility construction in areas without established concrete supply stations.

Emergency & Disaster Recovery

Quick deployment for post-disaster reconstruction where infrastructure needs to be rebuilt rapidly.

Rental & Short-Term Projects

Ideal for concrete rental businesses serving multiple short-duration projects where moving a stationary plant is uneconomical.

How It Works

The mobile plant is delivered to site complete on its chassis. After positioning and leveling, the operator connects power and water supply. The PLC system guides through calibration. Within 1-3 days, the plant is producing quality concrete. When the project ends, the plant folds up and moves to the next site — typically within 1-2 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast can a mobile plant be installed?

Most mobile plants are operational within 1-3 days of arrival on site. No concrete foundation is required — just level ground.

What capacity mobile plants does YUDA offer?

Our standard mobile plant produces 25m³/h. For larger requirements, please inquire about our HZS stationary series which covers 25-480m³/h.

Can the mobile plant work in cold weather?

Yes. Optional cold-weather package includes heated water tank, insulated pipes, and antifreeze protection for reliable operation in temperatures down to -20°C.

Is CE certification available?

Yes. All YUDA mobile usina de concretos are CE certified and comply with European safety standards. ISO 9001:2015 quality management applies to our entire production line.

What's the difference between forced type and drum type mobile plants?

YUDA offers two mobile plant types. The forced type (this page) uses a JS twin-shaft mixer for high-quality concrete. The trailer drum type uses a self-falling drum mixer, is more compact, and can be towed by a standard truck — ideal for very remote or small-scale projects.
